I Thought It Was A Good Deed

June 13, 2025 Random

Just a few minutes ago, I re-read a list of writing prompts from a website. There’s one about ‘something found on the sidewalk’ that summoned a memory from the back of my head. Not that I recall finding something peculiar on the sidewalk, instead, my mind highlighted the memory of putting something on the side of the road. Are you curious about what the thing was? Either way, let me tell the story.

So, I used to walk home on a regular basis. Honestly, I’m doubting my memory about this right now. This thing happened either when I used to walk home from the public transport’s stopping point during middle school, or when I walked home from elementary school which was fairly nearby. The key of this sidewalk object was a plastic cup of water.

Back in those days, I somewhat often held a cup of water on the way home. Then as I walked home, I would stop for a while at one turning point. That turning point was like an entry to a small hallway I liked to pass by to get home. At that particular small location, an idea came to my mind: “What if I just leave this half-full cup of water here? Perhaps someone thirsty will pass by, then they can drink it.” Yes, that was a bit delusional. I genuinely thought it was a good deed, as if I was helping people by putting my water on that nowhere. As I remember, I kept doing that for a while and have no idea when and why I stopped.

Recently, something reminded me of that weird habit I considered as a kindness. And yeah again, I forgot what reminded me. Is my memory getting worse, or what? Hopefully no. Anyway, I had a moment of thinking through that action as an older human. Not it seemed like a totally irrelevant thing to be considered a good deed. Like, what are the chances of someone would randomly take a half-full cup of water on the roadside? Even if there was, a thirsty person who saw the cup, it’s very unlikely that they’d actually drink from it.

Thinking of it now has made me think that I might have been doing the opposite of good: I practically left a ‘trash’ on the road side. Although, yeah, I put it neatly, haha. But still, I bet those cups ended up being trashes someone else needed to take care of.

So, to anyone who threw my cups in the right place back then—I’m sorry. From the little girl who left them there, she innocently thought she was doing the right thing. Her intention was to help someone, turned out, all she did was mostly making litter.
