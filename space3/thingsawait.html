Beyond The Things That Wait

June 16, 2025 Random

In a world where time is a thing, one thing can either be in the past, present, or future. As we understand, anything that is happening or existing right now is a part of the present. All those ‘now’ things are the ones which we would mostly notice, and actually pay attention to. Sometimes, our focus to anything that is happening in front of our eyes at the moment even unintentionally leaves other things unnoticed. One example is; the things that wait. So, what exactly do I mean by the things that wait?

Actually, we can interpret it in many directions. In this life, there are always things that are waiting. These things may not explicitly affect our life at the moment—or even left unnoticed. Yet, they might hold precious meaning. Personally, to me, knowing that there are things await gives me a peculiar sense of joy—or even better, relief. Let me walk you through this notion with a few examples.

A few years ago, a friend of mine gave me a birthday gift. The gift was a starter plant kit with a small cardboard box. The box contains plant seeds, a pot, soil, fertilizer, and even a caring tips note. Such a unique and exciting gift to use, isn’t it? As happy as I was to receive the gift, I’ve never tried to plant the seeds for some reasons to this day. Even so, I always love the idea of having seeds waiting to be planted. Although I’m not sure whether they can still grow after years, I always feel like those seeds are still waiting for me.

Well, that plant kit gift obviously isn’t the only thing that wait I’ll mention. I have other examples, which I think, are even more relatable: books waiting to be read. I guess it’s a universal problem, isn’t it? People tend to buy books without reading them immediately. Some even ended up buying some more before finishing the previous ones. Hence, some people would have a pile of books waiting to be read. So do I! Not a pile, though—just a few, hehe. But, having one or two books waiting to be read is probably enough for me to understand why people apparently enjoy having books waiting for them. I’ve come to believe that we may like the thought of the are things waiting for us. In this case, we are excited to know we will still have things to read when the boring time arrives. Apart from that, yeah—books are always somewhat tempting to buy.

Anyway, these two are just some simple concrete examples. If we dive deeper into our thoughts of this, we’ll realize that there are even more things await that we shall be glad for. Like, places waiting to be visited, nice people waiting to meet us, ice creams waiting to be tasted. Or as simple as tomorrow, waiting patiently to be lived.

Although, on the other hand, awaiting things could be worrying or scary as well. For instance, we are likely to have tasks waiting to be done, chores to be completed, or serious chats waiting to be replied. These kinds of things in the waiting list can make us feel uneasy whatsoever.

So, as my personal conclusion to this piece, things that wait can be exciting, scary or even both. But what’s actually important is the way we perceive them. We can turn both those exciting and scary things in the waiting line into a motivation to do our best in the present. Let’s make sure that their waiting is worth it.
