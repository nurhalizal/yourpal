Little Art Out of Boredom

May 24, 2025 Random

Another day of talking about my childhood memory. I’m just gonna recount a memory of a little me doing things out of boredom. I’ve always liked doing random things, even as a kid. Or… I possibly was even more creative at finding random things to do back then. By creative, I indeed mean I used to try creating or making things. From things as common as making origami to sewing ‘leftover’ fabrics. I want to tell you about something in between.

So, my front neighbour used to have Ashoka tree inside their fence. The branches were spread passing the fence—thus we could pick the flowers from outside the house. My friends and I occasionally played around that tree, picked some flowers and leaves to play with. Typically, we’d pick some flowers then arranged them to be accessories; necklaces, bracelets, or even a little ring. We did that by connecting one flower to another until it reached our aimed size of accessories. To be specific, we tucked the end of each stalk to the hole at the center of the petals. Yeah, it’s quite difficult to explain. But I honestly assume that my friends and I aren’t the only ones who used to make these stuffs. So, let’s just consider people will get it.

Talking of making things using the Ashoka flowers, I remember a ‘unique’ memory of me and those flowers. As I said, I normally made accessories out of the flowers with my friends. However, I didn’t always have some friends around when I wanted to spend some time ‘playing’.

One day, I was bored—not doing anything for a while. Yeah, throwback to those times where my mind was freer, haha. I was in front of my house, staring directly at my front neighbour’s house. Yup, the tree with its bright yellow flowers was the main view I saw. I don’t remember how I came up with it, but I had the idea to make some ‘art’ using the flower. Thus, I picked some and brought it to the front of my house. Along with those flowers, I took a leftover cardboard and some glue. I then gently plucked the petals from the stalks. Following that, I glued petal by petal into the cardboard.

After spending some time on it, I finally managed to complete my proud art—a petal painting, I call it. I know it’s gonna sound silly to call it an art, but I want to anyway. Oh yeah, let me describe what I made. It was just a ‘picture’ of a house, like the ones kids would draw the first time they learn to draw something. The unique thing was, instead of pencil or ink, the outline of the picture was the Ashoka flower petals.

At the moment, I innocently thought I just made a very creative ‘art’. I was probably like, “Who would’ve ever thought to draw using the Ashoka petals? And I did it!” After I finished it, I proudly showed the petal painting to my parents. I forgot how they reacted, but in my memory, I did get some appreciation for my ‘creativity’! To this day, that simple memory of being proud of a small thing I did stays well in my memory. Shout out to my younger self who gave enough credit and appreciation to herself. The current and future me need to learn from it; better appreciate every little good thing I do.

Ps: Need to learn to proudly tell my family all things I’ve done well too, just as I used to.
