Quirky Thing I Made Up

June 7, 2025 Random

Just earlier today, my parents were cleaning and tidying up some stuffs. In the middle of it, my mother found some old books of mine in a messy condition. Apparently, the cardboard box we used to store them had gotten damp. Therefore, I was asked to tidy those books up again and put them into a new box. If you know me, I’d be eager to have a chance to check out my old belongings.

Given the chance, I didn’t just tidy up those books again. Instead, I spent some time checking them out. I opened almost all of the books, turned some pages, and even put some thoughts on what I was seeing written on those books. After looking at several books, I noticed something; some of those books had scribbles written in quite unique letters. Neither Latin nor Arabic, and not even Greek. But those letters seemed familiar to me. Those scribbles—which were mostly my name—were written in ‘my’ alphabet letters. Sound silly, huh?

Here’s the story. When I was in elementary school, I somehow had the idea of making my own alphabet letters. I wanted to have something like that in order to be able to write anything without other people understanding. Looking back, I think it was my child-self’s desire to be mysterious. Eventually, I wrote a full alphabet of those made-up letters. They were actually taken from the Latin alphabet as we use in English and Bahasa Indonesia, but I modified them as well as trying to give all those letters common characteristics as a signature; they’re kind of angular.

Back then, I was genuinely proud to complete the made-up alphabet codes. I used to enjoy writing with those letters; I wrote about things that bothered me, like grudges I had that time, and mostly, I wrote my name. So, if you check my old books—especially the ones from elementary school, you’d find those unique writings somewhere.

Nowadays, I no longer use those letters as much; almost never anymore, actually. I guess it might seem childish to use those made-up letters again—but part of me still finds it charming. But come to think of it; I still have the habit of writing random things on papers while intending that people shouldn’t understand what I write. Perhaps the idea of using the made-up letters is not a bad idea at all.

Anyway, thanks to my child-self for having enough creativity and ‘strange’ courage to make up a signature set of letters that I now cherish.
