Unforgettable Ride Home

June 26, 2025 Random

On the other day, I went to a mall with my friends by a public transportation called bajaj (read: bajai) and before we even entered the mall, we already talked about how are we gonna go back. As we were talking about transportations, I was reminded of several unforgettable public transportation experiences. There’s a particular story that I ended up recounting a little to my friends that day—yes, before we even entered the mall. To my surprise, they reacted in a way I didn’t expect. Their reaction made me realize that the story is unforgettable for a reason.

In order to get you out of curiosity, I’m gonna talk about the story in details (I hope). Started from middle school, I became used to using public transportations to go home from school. Usually, I needed two types of transportations—one is called pete-pete and the other one could be anything to take me to a certain location to get the pete-pete. For your information, pete-pete is a medium sized public car with a certain route for each. This public car doesn’t normally pass in front of my schools—both middle school and high school. Hence, I needed to go to a certain place, which is usually on the roadside of the main street to wait for the car to pass by. For most of the times, I was with my friends whenever I used that transport and there was rarely any problem with it. Even if we did get into trouble about it, at the very least we were with each other.

However, during high school, I found myself having to go home alone. Fortunately, everything generally went well. But one day, I was waiting at the same spot for the transport when I found out that there was some issue about that typical transport. I saw several cars passing by in front of me but only a few of them stopped. And those who stopped were either on different route from I needed or the drivers said that they wouldn’t reach my stop. At that moment, I almost gave up on it and texted one of my friends to order online transport for me. Yeah, I didn’t even have any online transport apps on my phone back then. But as I was at the edge of giving up on the public car transport, another one stopped in front of me—giving me hope. The driver let me hop onto the car, and so I did. I thought to myself, “Finally, a smooth ride home.”

But little did I know—that wishful thinking faded in a second when the car stopped right in the middle of my way home. I was like, “What!? I thought this one could take me home!” Anyway, as upset as I was that time, I had no choice but to get off of the car along with the other passengers. That car left us on the roadside next to a nearly shabby bus stop. It was very inconvenient to me. I couldn’t ask anyone to take me there, I already cancelled asking my friend to order an online transport for me, and as I said, I didn’t have the online transport app whatsoever. I felt like I only had one option left; talk and ask for help to a stranger.

Eventually, I gathered my courage to talk to one of my fellow passengers who was still there at the bus stop. I tried my best to politely ask if she could help me order an online transport. Thankfully, she agreed even though she seemed to be in the middle of ordering one too. Without needing to wait for long, my transport arrived. I finally truly could go home conveniently thanks to that stranger woman. Recounting this story reminds me of how much a small kindness can actually affect others. It’s sad that I can’t remember anything about her, but her kindness stays forever in my memory.

By the way, there’s another meaning of the story I just realized after telling my friends the other day. My friends’ reaction that I mentioned earlier was basically; “Woah, you’ve grown up!” Hearing that, it made me recognize that this experience was probably one of my ‘growing up’ moments. Come to think of it, had I not been at least a little mature, I would have just cried at the bus stop.
