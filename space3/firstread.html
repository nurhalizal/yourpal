First Reading Book I Got

date Random

I went to a bookstore (inside a mall) with some of my friends recently. While exploring the bookstore, one of them called me to look around the children section of the bookstore. She said that she wanted to buy one for her little sister but ended up not getting any. Perhaps nothing matched her taste yet. But at least, I’ve genuinely supported her to actually buy one at first. Little did she know, that moment reminded me of something; the first book I ever got. I actually did carry on to tell her about this partly, so I’m going to talk about it more here.

By the way, the first book that I’m talking about here is a random reading book aside from my school books. So, this book was one of those small meaningful gifts I ever received. As I remember, my brother was the one who bought me that book. The book had a green cover with the title, “Collection of Nusantara Folktales/\textit{Kumpulan Cerita Rakyat Nusantara}” written on it. Yeah, the book included folktales from every province in Indonesia which was very interesting. Not just that, the book was colorful with an illustration for every story within. I was totally thrilled to get that book and was eager to start reading it. I suppose I managed to read every story of the book and those stories were all awesome and full of morals.

Before reading that book, I reckon that I didn’t even know lots of Indonesian folklores. So, thanks to that book and my brother who got it for me, some classic Indonesian folklores are stored in my memory as well. Looking back, the stories also opened my eyes to appreciate Indonesian cultures too—the appreciation we’re probably lacking nowadays. Sadly, now the book itself is currently undetected at my house. Yet, as I got it when I was still an elementary school student, I believe it’s still safely kept in a box along with my elementary school books. Hopefully that’s true.

Now I guess you can see why I highly encouraged my friend to buy one for her little sister. It's because I had been in the position of that little sister. I imagined my friend’s little sister being so happy to get a gift—especially a book—from her sister. Just as happy as I was back then. Besides, I also figured that a gift like that would stay as a good memory. Yes, simply like how I still remember about that folklore book even though I got it from my brother years ago.

Sometimes, the one gift that doesn’t seem to mean anything can turn out to be one of the best ones the receiver ever got.
