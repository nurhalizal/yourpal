The Seat I Always Prefer

May 30, 2025 Random

On my first day of elementary school, it wasn’t just the students who got hyped up to pick a seat. Instead, the parents were even more eager. I realized that taking a seat is considered a crucial thing. Overtime, I’ve come to agree to the notion even more. That day, as I recall, my mother helped to pick a seat for me and I just sat there. As the time passes by, I’ve come to many situations that require taking a seat—by myself. Even when I was still in elementary school, as we passed to the next grades, I needed to take a seat like every new term.

Back then, many things influence my seat preference and some still do today. One of the typical seats I prefer to take in classes; is the front row seat. Especially the one that’s not exactly in the center, yet not preferably in the corner. However, I’ve ended up sitting at the most front row in the corner. Which is also fine, hehe. Well, I like taking a front seat simply because I intend to focus in the class, and I actually enjoy some advantages of the front row. For instance, sitting there has allowed me to communicate indirectly with the teacher or lecturer at times. Later on, at least one teacher/lecturer has mentioned the seat choice in class sometimes can tell the student’s character. It confirmed that taking a seat matters.

The position of the seat in respect to the class isn’t the only thing that influences my seat preference. The people around me matter as well. Besides looking for a strategic seat, I also consider who would sit around me. Sitting around those friends I’m close to has always been enjoyable. We can have a little chat, ask each other something, or even tell a subtle joke, hehe.

But, on the first day of something—like first day of middle school and college—I barely knew anyone. So, seat position was my only consideration. Just like the first time I entered my fixed class in middle school. I abruptly took a seat in the front row, next to another girl who was already there before I arrived. I didn’t care who she was; all I cared about that I wanted to take that nice strategic seat. Then, here’s the little twist; she’s now one of my closest friends ever.

That’s crazy how my seat preference has led me to one of my best friendships. So yeah, taking a seat does matter.
