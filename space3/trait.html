Though Hidden, The Trait Stays

July 11, 2025 Random

Throughout my six-year elementary school period, I used to be a rather cranky person. I easily got angry, probably acted grumpy, not to mention that I also might have acted bossy in my class—which I came to regret later. Unfortunately, I didn’t it in time until the end of my elementary school years, perhaps even after graduation. Slowly, I became almost fully aware that my friends might have been hating me this whole time, which I truly feel bad about. I then started regretting having got mad at my friends, scolded them in some ways, and other bad behaviours of mine back then. Sadly, we can’t turn back in time and all I can do is wishing them to forgive me.

However, it was obviously not the end of everything—including my personal growth and image. I’m not sure if I remember correctly, but I’m convinced that I told myself back then to change as I started middle school. I must not be that cranky girl in the class anymore, whatsoever.

Over the years, I guess things turned out pretty good. I didn’t feel like getting mad a lot to my friends, since middle school. Even better, I’ve heard one or two friends mentioning or asking how would I be when I get mad since they thought I rarely get angry. That kind of comments is actually surprising to me, knowing myself as an actually irritable person within. But that’s a good sign, isn’t it? I felt reassured that I managed to control myself a bit better.

Well, perhaps at least until that one time I felt like my grumpy personality came back. It happened when I was involved in a group work with some of my friends. Unlucky for some of us, I found one of my group mates to be, well,… annoying. Can’t explain how and why, but at some points, I just couldn’t stand to be patient. And that, is how my ‘anger’ arose, to the point that even my other friends were aware that I turned into a kind of irritable person. Yeah, another negative trait of mine was revealed to my circle of friends. It was alright, though, they could clearly see the reason why I was upset.

To my friends, that angry behaviour of mine was just a temporary reaction that happened because something triggered it. Yet to me, it hit me more than that. I figured that all this time I felt like I became calmer or somewhat alike—was not really because I lost my anger within.

Apparently, the calmer me was a result of a better self-control and better environment that didn’t trigger me to get mad. I never really lost my anger sense at all; my environment just probably grows healthier for me and I’m trying my best to cope with this cranky personality that I consider as an issue. So yeah, the grumpy me is ‘till there, just not always visible.

Ps: After graduating elementary school, I guess I did say sorry to one or some of my friends about my past behaviours. And if I remember correctly, a friend actually thanked me for that, saying that they understand I got mad or scolded them for a reason. It’s truly a relief to her. So, to all my friends and beloved people around me, a genuine apology from this imperfect self who gets mad a lot!
