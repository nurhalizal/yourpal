Setting Alarms

May 10, 2025 Random

Time is absolutely one significant thing. Regardless of how we perceive time in detail, I bet everyone will agree that time is a precious matter. Time needs to be managed, regulated, and spent in the best possible way. People in general aim for that. To achieve the ‘managed time’, we attempt several different ways. Some like to make a written schedule for everything they be doing in a whole day. Some also like to make a to-do list, to remind them what they need to get done with.

There are many other ways, I believe. But, out of different ways of managing our time, there’s a basic way most people use as a part of their time management. It is setting alarms. This action doesn’t seem as a significant part of time management. Yet in my opinion, it is. We commonly set alarms to wake us up in the morning, and I’m telling you; it’s the start of everything you’ll be going through for the rest of the day. Therefore, setting alarms is a significant part of our time management.

I want to share more about setting alarms. The reason is because I use alarms more than that. It’s almost like I utilize alarms more for my waking time than to wake me up. I don’t know how and when it began, but I’ve eventually become used to setting alarms at random times. Well yeah, not totally random since I always a certain reason each time. Currently, other than my waking up alarm, I have a daily alarm to remind me of a small routine.

People—including my family find it weird, because the alarm is set around the time people should’ve been in bed. Sometimes I tell them it’s a go-to-sleep reminder, haha. Besides that, I also occasionally set alarm for the next one hour, two hours, or even just thirty minutes. I set alarms like that to remind me that I have things to do and I shall start by that time the alarm rings. Although, I set them without written context. So, whenever my phone alarm rings, I’ll be like, “Oh yeah, I’ve got things to do… hmm, but what, eh?” I guess things like that have happened, but don’t worry about me it doesn’t always happen. I normally remember my to-do-list.

There are even times where I started before the alarm rings. It’s like just by knowing that an alarm is coming already makes me feel cautious and it apparently triggers me to start early. I guess it’s the alarm that starts working early as well. It affects me before the real effect should arrive.

Writing this makes me realize that setting alarm is a small yet powerful action. It’s unwittingly working hard to send caution and reminder for us.
