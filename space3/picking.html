Picking Things Randomly

June 20, 2025 Random

Remember that I mentioned keeping such random objects, like candlenut and seashell? That particular habit of keeping uncanny objects is apparently the result of another quirky habit of mine. Over the time, I’ve come to realize that I like picking random things from the ground. And I really mean it. I didn’t realize it before until recently, I picked another object from the ground and it’s in my bag right now. So yep, I decided I want to keep it.

My friends and I were walking from our campus to one of their places nearby. While walking, still in the campus area, we saw a whole cashew fruit on the ground—having both parts: the cashew apple and cashew nut. We didn’t even realize that there was a cashew tree until we saw the fruit and looked up to see that the tree was actually there all along.

Anyway, we were all curious about the cashew since it’s quite rare for us (or just for me) to see the whole cashew fruit right in front of our eyes. Soon enough after picking the fruit, I detached the cashew nut from its apple—eventually threw away the cashew apple—and kept the cashew nut! Following that event, I realized that I indeed enjoy randomly picking things from the ground. By that means, the cashew nut isn’t the only thing I’ve picked.

As I mentioned before, I keep a seashell I picked from a beach. Well, this one isn’t probably that quirky since people do pick seashells—some even do that for a living, I suppose. But I also mentioned having a candlenut saved in my small box. I picked that one year ago from the ground when I was back in my mother’s hometown. The town is like 5 hours away and I took that candlenut all the way home. It didn’t stop there; I actually keep it up till now.

Other than those particular nuts and seashell, there are some more usual objects I’m used to pick from the ground. Like the nuts, they’re also parts of plants. One of which is something that look like a seed of certain trees. I’ve found those seeds a lot probably since high school, but I still have no idea what it is. It’s like every time I thought of scanning it to browse, I didn’t end up actually doing it. I don’t always keep this type of seeds like the candlenut and cashew nut, but I have picked them numerous times. Not just picking them, I like to ‘peel’ them, ‘cracked’ them open, and even somehow ‘carved’ them with a needle following a certain path of their surface. I like doing that whenever I feel bored, so I keep picking those seeds when I found some. I think to myself that I might need them to kill some time later.

Nowadays, I started to think that it’s a quirky habit that I’m not sure what led me to have it. It’s probably a mixture of curiosity, creativity, and an absolute quirk.
