A Subtle Achievement to Relish

June 6, 2025 Random

I initially wrote this piece to answer this question: "What's the most recent thing you did alone and you enjoyed?" My answer to this question might not be the typical answer you expected. But I gladly want to answer with this; a presentation. Throughout school and now college, most of the presentation assignments I’ve ever got are group tasks. For almost all of the tasks, I needed to discuss and divide the materials/jobs with my groups. This way of finishing the task seems to be the best way to do it. I personally don’t need to do a lot; I just focused on my part and let others handle theirs.

But honestly, sometimes I got a little issue with this kind of group tasks/presentations. To put it simply, I usually have my own ‘goals’ or like ‘expectations’ for how the finished assignment should turn out. But when doing a group task/presentation, I can’t just enforce those expectations of mine to my groups. In my head, it would seem rude and unappreciative. A few weeks ago, I got an individual presentation task. Well, we could form a group up to three members under certain circumstances—but most of us just went individually. So did I.

The task was to present a paper of our choice. At first, I was almost overwhelmed just by thinking of doing the task. I kinda picked the paper randomly and didn’t understand what it’s all about. Yet I moved on anyway. I started reading and learning the paper (mostly with GPT’s assistance, so thanks!). As I dived into the paper, I began finding it fascinating. I’m not saying it became so easy, though. Nonetheless, I became eager to do the best for this task. I continued learning the paper, making a note (using LaTeX which I’m also delighted to be able to), then obviously, creating the presentation.

The amount of time I spent on this presentation was much more compared to any other I’ve done in a group. Besides, I never really practiced a presentation properly—but for this one, I even recorded myself practicing it! Long story short, I finished the presentation in time and was able to present it well. It felt wonderful to finally be able to complete a presentation according to my ‘expectations’. I know it didn’t go perfectly, but I suppose it was something I did pretty good—and I did all on my own.
