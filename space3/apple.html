About Apples

April 6, 2025 Random

Apples… one interesting kind of fruit. I assume that it’s one of the most well-known fruits all over the world. When you try to think of a fruit—unless you just ate another kind—I suppose apples will appear in your head first. Now I’m trying to sort out the reasons why apples are so iconic.

First of all, I think the name has something to do with it. These very fruits are known as ‘apples’ in English—the most spoken language in the world. Besides, some other languages have a similar word for apples. For instance, in my language, Indonesian, the fruits are called ‘apel’. As these words started with an ‘A’—the first letter of the Latin alphabet—this kind of fruit is likely to come to our minds when we think of fruits. Now, I have a hypothesis that we tend to think alphabetically when it comes to random list of things, haha.

Well, the name isn’t the only reason, of course. I believe that the round-but-curved shape, especially with commonly red color also affects its peculiarity. To me, the shape of apples kinda describe what it takes to be considered fruits, and red is a striking color. Hence, apples become particularly memorable. This kinda brings us to the next reason I can’t think of. The image of an apple is well-recognized possibly because the fruits’ worldwide growth. Apples grow in many regions, so they’re basically known by all populations across the globe.

Then, besides the few reasons I’ve sorted out, I reckon that a religious or cultural exposure also plays a role in this. In Abrahamic religions, we’re taught a story of Adam and Eve that involves a fruit—which is commonly said to resembles an apple. This story even resulted the term, “Adam’s apple”. Anyway, people may have different opinion about all I’ve written here, though.

By the way, it’s already a long piece just talking about apples, isn’t it? But I still want to add a bit more. Other than talking about Apples being an iconic type of fruits, I’mma share a personal ‘feeling’ on apples. Since I was very little, I’ve always considered apples as my favorite type of fruits. I even have it written in my old binder notebook. That sentence written about apple is also a proof of the beginning of my English learning. I suppose it’s one of my first ever written english sentences.

Back to the apples, to this day, the sentence remains valid, apples are indeed one of my favorites. Furthermore, I personally conclude that Apples might not be the king of fruits, but I suppose that they're the icon of the kingdom.
