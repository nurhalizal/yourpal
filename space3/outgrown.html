Outgrown, Not Thrown

June 23, 2025 Random

By now—the time I’m writing this, I only graduated high school not too long ago. So, it’s to no one’s surprise that I still keep my high school uniforms neatly. But what if I tell you that I still have at least one of my elementary school uniforms stored in my wardrobe? From some other points of view, it hasn’t seemed that long since I graduated elementary school. Yet for me—especially in the context of keeping school uniforms—over five years is a pretty long time. Even though I haven’t grown as much, I obviously have outgrown my elementary school uniforms.

However, throwing every piece of the school uniforms away was never an option for me. Actually, there are school uniforms I no longer keep for the reason that I have some relatives younger than me and went to the same school as I did. So, we gave them some of my school uniforms to wear as I didn’t necessarily need it anymore. Yet, fortunately I still got a few left—some shirts, sports uniform, and skirts. Now, those school uniforms I have left, from elementary school to high school, are wrapped in one plastic bag which I put in my wardrobe.

Keeping the school uniforms may seem silly or even unnecessary as it’s not like we will wear them as much again. But, as I always say, every object has its own story that is worth preserving. Those school uniforms were basically once my daily outfit. Perhaps, I even used to wear them for longer time than any other clothes I had back then. I would even say that at the moment, they were a part of my identity. The people I’ve met through my school days saw me mostly (or even only) in those uniforms. It’s even possible that I’ve encountered some people who don’t even remember me now, but may still remember meeting a girl in a certain school uniform. Consequently, looking at my school uniforms after years; it feels as if those pieces of clothing can tell me a whole story which they and I have been through.

As time passing by, changes have happened to them. They’re certainly not as fresh as they were. Some spots have had their colors faded. Not to mention the size of them that I have outgrown, gladly haha. But in spite of all that, I intend to keep them for as long as possible. I guess, every now and then I better looked back at them, and tell myself, “You’ve grown so much! You’d been through a lot with them, and there are more stories waiting to be unfolded.”
