Memory Keepers

March 1, 2025 Random

Every moment will eventually be a memory. Throughout our days, we must have bound to a moment we wish had never happened. But because we could never undo something, we’d at least hope that the memory of it wouldn’t stay. As a simple example, when you accidentally mistook someone else for someone you know. You’d wish both of them a nd you to forget the moment instantly. In contrary, there are also moments we’ve faced in life that we wish to stay forever. Like, when we visit a very fascinating place, we’d wish the moment, or at least the memory to last forever. So, how do we keep a memory? We obviously need memory keepers. Then, what could be the memory keepers?

Well, memory keepers can simply be our own brains, our own minds. But the problem is, we can’t ‘see’ what’s inside our brains, so there’s no guarantee that the memories are kept, haha. Moreover, even though our brains do remember the memories, we always need concrete objects to remind us of memories—or in other words, to help us ‘keep’ them.

There are many things we can consider memory keepers. Well, basically anything can be one. Every object may have at least a single special memory within. A keychain may remind us to the moment we got it, or the memory of the person who gave us. A street we used to pass by during a certain period will get us back to the moments we passed the street. There are even many more examples of things as memory keepers. However, among any objects that can keep memory, I’ve got two that I consider the most effective memory keepers. The first one is a piece of writing. Writing about the moments we want to cherish is delightful thing. Not only that we’d find joy in writing them, anytime later when we read it, the writing will narrate the memory kind of exactly as it was. It’s even possible to feel the emotions in it all over again just by reading. Yet, there’s one thing that a piece of writing lacks of; visualization of the memories. This leads us to my next top two memory keepers—pictures/photos. Pictures are likely wordless, but seeing a picture of the moments will somehow tell us about the memories. A picture of ourselves in an occasion can tell us where we were, whom we’re with, what we wore, and even how we felt.
