One Time My Respect For Someone Faded

July 4, 2025 Random

Genuinely, I wasn’t sure to write about this in the first place as it involves a rather negative memory of someone else. But it came to my mind and I just felt like I should write it down. So, here’s the story. One day during middle school, my friends and I were chatting in our classroom as usual. Somehow, the conversation leads to us talking about cheating for exams. If I’m not mistaken, one of us said something like, “What’s a good way to cheat, eh?” Perhaps, they were trying to look for a way to get away in the upcoming exams with cheating or simply just joking around.

Regardless of the motive behind that question, another friend of mine replied. Hearing her response, her words instantly became one of my favorite quotes I’ve ever heard directly. She casually said, “There’s no good way of cheating.” That one sentence struck me to the core. To me, it sounded like she basically said that there’s no such thing as a good way of cheating because cheating itself isn’t good at all. And I completely agree with that. My respect towards her climbed up really quick at that one moment—moreover she’s already a good friend of mine that I respected too, obviously.

After that, I guess the conversation continued normally and our exam started a few days later. When we had an exam, the seat arrangement was usually rearranged. My day-to-day seat in class was quite far from her, yet for the exam, I remember sitting around her—probably even exactly next to her. With that being said, I could basically see what she was doing. Not to mention that I am used to doing nothing in the middle of an exam if I’m stuck or just need some pause. So yeah, I did the same thing that day.

Once upon a day during an exam, I stopped and looked around a little—just around my own seat for absolutely no real reason. At that moment,… I unintentionally witnessed something; she opened a book which she apparently had put in the drawer under her table. Of course, my instinctive conclusion was; she was cheating—and I honestly felt down seeing that. It might be not a big deal for some, yet for me it was outrageous to witness. That friend whom I respected for saying that cheating isn’t good whatsoever—caught red-handed by my own eyes.

Yeah, I dramatically felt betrayed at that very moment, yet I suppose it shouldn’t ruin anything between us. She remains one of my good friends although we don’t get in touch a lot after graduating middle school. Besides, there’s also no guarantee that she was indeed cheating and perhaps if she did, she must have her own reasons back then. I’m against cheating nevertheless, but I guess I wouldn’t be against my friend at all for that reason. As the saying goes, \textit{hate the action, not the person}.
