A Silly Moment of Being Proud

May 15, 2025 Random

I wanna share a short memory I have from high school. This one is not a big deal like those big moments people have as their core school memories. Instead, this one happened just on a regular day of school. Honestly, I’ve forgotten the exact details of what led this to happen. So, it was just a normal day at school—either it was a break or just a moment of waiting for the teacher to come in. All I remember is that everyone was just chilling and no teacher was in our classroom.

I forgot the reason, but at a point, my friends—or just one main person started giving riddles. The whole class then became engaged with the riddles. People were excited to answer and to know the answers. By the way, those riddles are the funny-tricky type that were meant as jokes as well. So, after hearing answer by answer, most of the times, we went dumbfounded and laughed.

Personally, I enjoy that moment. I love being in the class when everyone was engaged and hyped up to the same thing. I like the feeling of being united, haha. Besides, I also like riddles. Moreover, growing up, my father has told me plenty of riddles, including the ones with silly unbelievable answers. Because of that, I was quite familiar with some riddles my friend was asking. Now, let’s get in to the main part of the story.

In the middle of the riddle time, that classmate of mine who ‘led’ the riddle time asked this question, “What part of the elephant is the biggest?” My classmates went with various answers, like its ears (yeah, elephant had wide ears, but the answer’s wrong). I forgot any other wrong answers, hehe. Anyway, after several answers, no one got it right, so I decided to give it a try, by saying, “its cage/enclosure.” And yep, I got it right. They—including the one who gave the riddle were stunned that I got it right, and obviously laughed at the answer.

One of the possible reasons of why they were so surprised by it—no intention to brag by the way—is that they knew me as an ‘academic’ person. So, how could someone like me knew a random riddle’s answer. To be honest, I knew the answer almost instantly. Yet I stayed silent for a while and just watched others answering the question. I didn’t do that on purpose, I was just not that ‘loud’ in class level, and would rather let others speak. It’s like I had an image of being quite calm and had to keep it.

Honestly, I was really happy and proud of that moment, but why? It’s just a riddle. However, to me, it felt like a moment of showing another side of me—the joke-related-lover side! Just now, I also think that it also showed how much perception affects people. Apparently, I got all their shocked reaction simply because in their perception, I wouldn’t even answer such riddle.
