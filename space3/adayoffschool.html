The Day I Didn't Make It To School

June 9, 2025 Random

A group of my friends once had an idea of making a ‘life-update’ presentation when we were about to graduate high school. They made a list of several things we should include in the presentation; which was all about high school life. Obviously, we needed to present about the great memories/experiences we’d had throughout high school as well as the bad ones. I had a few things on my positive list, but hardly thought of a certain thing about any bad one. At last, I wrote about a day when I didn’t make it to school. There were probably more of inconveniences I faced in high school, but this one was probably the worst that it was worth writing on my top bad experience list.

It happened one Monday morning. I got ready to school early in the morning normally despite that it was raining heavily. The rain didn’t stop me from hitting the road with my father by motorbike as usual. But don’t worry, we wore raincoats and I wore sandals instead of my school shoes. In my thoughts that time, it was just a normal day during the rainy season. We might get wet a little, but it’d be fine as we arrived.

Unfortunately, things didn’t go as well as expected. Some parts of the road were already flooded, the traffic jams increased significantly. I checked my phone while I was on the way—under the rain—to see how my friends were doing. Turned out, they were also struggling and trapped in the rain and traffic jams. Yet, we didn’t give up and kept on going to the school—until at one point—the flood was no longer unbearable. Riding a motorbike on that road was apparently impossible. Like, even if we did try, the engine might stop before we arrived.

Thus, I took shelter in a roadside shop, along with others who were also trapped. Meanwhile, on the phone, my friends were busy chatting about where and how we were doing, and some also offered me to get in their cars to get to school. I initially wanted to take the offer. But the rain didn’t seem to stop any time soon and we even got information that the schools were likely to give us a free day due to the rain. So, after hours of waiting for the rain to subside but it didn’t, my father and I decided to turn around and go home. At that moment, my mood was totally ruined. A Monday I should’ve spent excitedly returning to school after a weekend turned into a rather chaotic morning.

Afterwards, I then realized something else. I was possibly not in my full mood to go to school that day. And trust me, I rarely feel that way. I had my reason, though. I had a wound on one of my soles. I couldn’t even walk normally but didn’t want to be absent from school as well ‘cause I thought it was no big deal.

So, I wondered if it was a coincidence that I couldn’t make it to school that day or is it actually a way for me to take a day off as I needed.

Ps: My wound wasn’t supposed to get wet yet that time. I vaguely remember that we got it covered with plastic before I left for school. But it still got wet and looked bad as I got home.
