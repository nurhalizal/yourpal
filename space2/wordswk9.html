"Although Small, Make Sure You Start"

September 14, 2025 Words Worth Keeping

Before the idea of writing consistently came across my mind, I thought I needed to try reading random articles every once in a while, as a part of my new habit. So, I looked up random articles on the internet. One of the first websites that Google gave me was \textit{jamesclear.com}. I opened it and scrolled down the article lists, then eventually read one. Soon enough, I was hooked up into reading the articles on the website. To me, the articles are very well-written, valuable in terms of lessons, and most importantly, relatable. Little did I know, it was an article website by the author of world-famous book, \textit{Atomic Habits}. No wonder the articles are that amazing!

Afterwards, I read a few more articles (not on the same day, btw) until I decided to register my email to the newsletter version. Since then, I’ve been getting a newsletter every week. Reading the newsletter is just as good as reading the articles—it always comes with something to take on deeply. In those short newsletters, I’ve encountered many sentences that I value so much that I even took screenshots to save them. I’ve always wanted to write about those words, and I guess today’s the day for the first one.

As you may know—because I mentioned it at the beginning of this piece—I’ve committed to write consistently since a few months ago. What I’ve been writing is nothing much (including this), yet I still find it extremely challenging at times. There have been days when I felt like I couldn’t come up with anything to write, or I felt like what I just wrote is meaningless, and even thought of giving up on this writing journey. When meddled with other things we do daily, being consistent really is a big challenge, moreover when it comes to one that involves creativity. It’s like sometimes, my mind just won’t work well to write anything. When facing a struggle like that, I began to think like, “What am I doing anyway? It’s not like what I write is big, nor am I sure it’s worth reading.” Wonders like that often led to, “Should I just stop? Or at least skip for days?”

Coincidentally, in the middle of those doubts, I happened to read a quote form jamesclear’s newsletter that altered my perspective. The core idea of the quote was that even the smallest step taken will bring us closer to the big goal than never starting does. In that quote, the author gives a few examples of the case, which one of them was, “Writing one sentence has more in common with writing a book than never writing one.” My mind possibly went, “Woah, what a sentence! That sounds so true and you better remember this!” Yes, of course, reading it instantly boosted my mood and spirit.

Moreover, still in the same newsletter, he also points out how early and small beginning sometimes feels meaningless. And yes, as you can see, it’s highly relatable for me—it matched perfectly with how I often felt lately. The fact that the idea came with a recognition of how people often feel in terms of starting a journey, made me connect more to the advice of the quote.

My courage accelerated significantly by the notion this quote brought. It made me see that what I’d been feeling is valid and normal, yet it’s no reason for me to give up on this journey. Instead, having started it is something I should be grateful for. It’s also related to another powerful sentence at the end of the quote, which said, “Start small, but make sure you start.”

Lately, I’m still struggling at times to keep up on my commitment, but these words have given me extra courage not to give up.
