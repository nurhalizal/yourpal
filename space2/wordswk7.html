Internal Feeling vs. External Feedback

August 17, 2025 Words Worth Keeping

Frankly speaking, I’m now completely lost about where did I hear or read these words I’m planning to write about. Earlier today I was just checking my phones to find something to write, then I came across this sentence. I happened to have taken notes of this particular sentence a few days ago by sending it to my own personal chat. Usually, I take this type of notes because I heard or read it somewhere and found it interesting or relatable. And most of the times, I would remember about the source of those sentences or even the situation that led me to writing them.

However, I barely have any clue about this one even though I just kept it as a note since last week. My only suspicion was I heard it from a podcast considering I’ve enjoyed watching one or two podcasts these days. But after trying to rewatch a part of a podcast I initially suspected to be the source, I’m still struggling to find the exact inspiration of these words. But yeah, let’s just set the ‘source’ aside and talk about the words.

In the message note I mentioned, there written: “Sometimes, it’s more about how we feel within instead of the external feedback.” Now that I read it once again, I’m going back to wishing I could relive the moment I wrote it as a note and how I felt at that moment. But then if I see it from another side, it’s a good thing that I have a chance to read it as if I read it for the first time again. It’s like I was given an opportunity to walk through my feeling about those words all over again. Turned out, I felt nothing special. Haha, just kidding—obviously, I found it unusually fascinating.

After re-reading the words a few times, I came to a conclusion that this sentence is such a universally relatable one. As I see it, it resonates with anything that we do as a human being. Perhaps without us realizing, some of us grow up being focused or driven to focus on the external feedback we get from what we do ever since we were little. “You did a great job!”; “You could’ve done better.”; “What you got wasn’t good enough”; “That’s so good/creative!” Yes, some (or most) of us have been constantly either longing or anticipating these types of utterances whenever we just did something—and yeah, I’m one of ‘us’.

I just reckon that I’ve been in such condition as well; I quietly waiting for others to give positive feedback on what I’ve done. But I guess I can’t blame myself or others about it. Well, that kind of condition does have positive effects; we’d feel motivated to do our best in every given chance. Yet in spite of that, it has its own inevitable twist as well. When we’re in the middle of something, we’re most likely to only focus on the result and the upcoming feedback from others.

As a result, we tend to overlook the significance of the process and our own feeling about it. I understand that putting our best endeavours towards things we do in order to get the best result possible is necessary. However, there are another aspect that’s just as important: asking ourselves what we feel about the whole process and result. And this is exactly what I think the quoted sentence reminds us about. It’s absolutely alright and completely reasonable to hope for great results and feedbacks, but forgetting to ‘feed’ ourselves on the way is unacceptable.

After all, it’s how we feel inside that contributes more to our growth in every step we take—not what people out there say about it. Moreover, no one knows better about us and what we’ve been through than ourselves. Take their words as a motivation to grow more, not as an indicator whether we’ve made it or not.

Ps: I’ve found it! As I was writing, I eventually remember where I heard the sentence from. It was a typical inspirational/motivational clip I found when scrolling YouTube. I didn’t 100% agree with the whole clips, but I found my thought aligned in agreement with that very sentence from the clip.
