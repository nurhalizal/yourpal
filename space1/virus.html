Is Virus Alive?

September 21, 2025 Science Of Everyday Awe

About five years ago, the world was ‘shaken’ due to the existence of microscopic agent that we all know as: coronaviruses. The vast spread of these infectious agents caused the historic disaster; the CoViD-19 pandemic. The era costed us a lot of change that nowadays some even feel like the world has never been the same following the pandemic. While we’re aware that this type of viruses is dangerous, some may still lack of understanding what they actually are. What is a virus, anyway? Are they alive? Is it similar with bacteria? Or what?

Well, seemingly, even scientists are still not sure whether they’re living organisms or not. But they do know what they are, and have valid reasons behind the ‘doubt’. As we can grasp from the pandemic, viruses have genetic components and have the capability to replicate or we may call it reproduce. Surely that’s a typical living organisms’ behaviour. However, viruses don’t always hold onto that ability; there’s a particular requirement for virus to activate the ability to replicate. So, do you know what it is? Yes, a host cell.

Some people often compare them with bacteria as they’re both microscopic things that can cause disease to human or other living organisms. Yet, they’re not the same. Bacteria are cellular organisms, meaning they can perform cellular function including metabolism.

Meanwhile, viruses do not consist of cells. They can’t perform cellular function, provide energy, nor replicate by themselves. Basically, as one, they’re not alive. But then, when they have a living host cell, they gain the capability of being ‘alive’. They’ll start to make copies of themselves by taking advantages of their host cells.

Gradually, the host cells will be ‘full’ of copies of the virus which later cause the cells to burst. This attack to the host cells is what triggers the body’s immune systems causing us to get sick. Yeah, apparently, viruses are barely alive—yet when they are, they’re capable of doing such damage to the living organisms.
