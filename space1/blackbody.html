Why Do We Avoid Black Clothes Under the Sun?

May 7, 2025 Physics of Everyday Wonder

“Don’t go out in the sun wearing black!” As a person growing up in tropical area, I’m so familiar with this sentence. I believe I’m not the only person who has heard that sentence being said. It can get us wonder like, “Does that even matter, though?”. Well, not-too-surprisingly, it does. We possibly aren’t always capable of observing/feeling it clearly, but wearing black clothes do make us feel warmer. Before you ask why, I’m already eager to explain the physics behind this phenomenon.

The sun emits very high intensity of electromagnetic radiation ranges, including the visible light—that we see as the sunlight—and even extends beyond the visible light spectra. The amount of radiation that reach the earth interact with every object on earth, including us and our clothes. The interaction between matter and electromagnetic waves varies. Electromagnetic waves can be transmitted, reflected, and absorbed by the matter. The amount of waves/energy reflected, transmitted, and absorbed depends on the characteristics of the matter.

In this context, we know a parameter called emissivity coefficient. This parameter tells how well a material can absorb and emit electromagnetic radiation compared to a blackbody. A low emissivity means that the material doesn’t absorb and emit well, instead it reflects most of the waves. On the contrary, high emissivity (near to one) means the material absorbs and emits the waves very well. And here’s the big point: among all visible colors, black has the highest emissivity, thus the perfect emitter/absorber is called blackbody.

Black color basically absorbs any electromagnetic wavelength. This blackbody radiation explains why we feel warmer wearing black under the sun. Although the black clothes we wear aren’t typically the perfect black that absorbs ‘everything’, they still absorb pretty high radiation compared to other colors. Consequently, we feel warmer wearing black as our clothes absorb more energy.

So, next time you plan to go out under the sun for long, better leave your black clothes behind.
